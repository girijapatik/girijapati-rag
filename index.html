Comprehensive Templating Guide

Learn how to format your MS Word (.docx) templates to harness the full power of LexiCore AI.

💡 Core Concept: LexiCore reads your standard Word documents and looks for specific tags wrapped in curly braces {like_this}. It uses the text inside the braces to understand what data to extract from your uploaded case files, and then replaces the tag with the generated answer.

1. Standard Fact Extraction

Use basic curly braces to ask the AI to find specific, short factual data (like names, dates, amounts, or short sentences) from your case files.

How you type it in Word:

This agreement is entered into on {Date_of_Agreement} between {Name_of_Plaintiff} and {Name_of_Defendant}.

How LexiCore generates it:

This agreement is entered into on 14th October 2023 between Rajesh Sharma and Acme Corp Ltd..

2. AI-Drafted Paragraphs & Sections

Prefix your tag with AI: to command the AI to generate long-form, intelligent, legally formatted paragraphs based on complex instructions.

How you type it in Word:

GROUNDS FOR INJUNCTION

{AI:Draft_three_paragraphs_explaining_the_grounds_of_urgency_based_on_the_breach_notice}

How LexiCore generates it:

GROUNDS FOR INJUNCTION

The Plaintiff respectfully submits that the Defendant's actions constitute a material breach of Clause 4.2...

Furthermore, the continuous infringement poses an immediate and irreparable harm to the Plaintiff's business operations...

3. Dynamic Lists & Numbering

To generate dynamic lists (where you don't know how many items there will be), wrap your line in a # loop tag and close it with a / loop tag.

Crucial Rule: Apply MS Word's native numbering (1, 2, 3...) ONLY to the middle line containing the content tag!

How you type it in Word:

{#AI:Generate_List_Of_Damages}

1. {Damage_Description} - {Amount}

{/AI:Generate_List_Of_Damages}

How LexiCore generates it:
1.Loss of revenue for Q3 - $45,000
2.Reputational damage - $100,000
3.Legal fees incurred - $12,500

4. Dynamic Tables (Chronologies)

You can populate native Word tables. Create a 2-row table. Put your headers in the top row. In the bottom row, open the loop in the first cell, and close it in the last cell.

How you type it in Word:
DateEvent DescriptionReference Page
{#AI:Build_Case_Chronology}
{Date}		 {Event} {Page_Num}
{/AI:Build_Case_Chronology}
How LexiCore generates it:
DateEvent DescriptionReference Page
10-Jan-22Notice sent to DefendantPg. 12
15-Jan-22Defendant replied denying claimsPg. 14
01-Feb-22Suit filed in High CourtPg. 1
